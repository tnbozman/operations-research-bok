# Navy Operations

## Introduction

Naval forces operate in the surface, subsurface, and littoral environments to achieve **sea control**, **power projection**, and **deterrence**. U.S. naval doctrine is codified in **NDP 1 (Naval Warfare)** and joint doctrine in **JP 3-32 (Command and Control for Joint Maritime Operations)**. Fleet commanders — numbered fleet commanders and carrier strike group (CSG) / amphibious ready group (ARG) commanders — task operational outcomes across the following primary operation types.

---

## Sea Control Operations

Sea control is the condition that exists when one has freedom of action to use an area of sea for one's own purposes for a period of time and, where necessary, can deny its use to an adversary.

| Operation Type | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Anti-Surface Warfare (ASuW)** | Detect, track, target, and engage enemy surface vessels using air, surface, and subsurface platforms |
| **Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW)** | Detect, track, and neutralize enemy submarines using a layered system of sensors, weapons, and platforms |
| **Mine Warfare (MIW)** | Lay or neutralize mines to control sea lines of communication; includes offensive mining and mine countermeasures (MCM) |
| **Submarine Barrier Operations** | Deploy submarines in a patrol line or area to intercept and prosecute enemy submarines or surface ships transiting a chokepoint or sea lane |
| **Maritime Interdiction Operations (MIO)** | Stop, search, and if necessary seize vessels suspected of carrying contraband, weapons, or persons in violation of UN resolutions or national law |
| **Escort and Convoy Operations** | Protect high-value vessels (e.g., logistics ships, amphibious shipping) from submarine, surface, and air threats during transit |

!!! note "OR Lens — Sea Control"
    Submarine barrier operations are a classic **search theory** problem. Optimal barrier geometry, transit probability, and detection probability are analyzed using random search models. Convoy escort optimization uses network flow and queueing models.

## Power Projection Operations

Power projection extends naval combat power ashore or threatens to do so in order to influence adversary behavior.

| Operation Type | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Carrier Strike Operations** | Employ carrier-based aviation to conduct strikes, air interdiction, close air support, and suppression of enemy air defenses |
| **Amphibious Operations** | Project landing forces from sea to shore to seize and hold objectives; includes assault, raid, demonstration, and withdrawal |
| **Naval Surface Fire Support (NSFS)** | Employ shipboard gun and missile systems to support forces ashore |
| **Tomahawk Land Attack Missile (TLAM) Strike** | Deep strike against fixed targets ashore using ship- or submarine-launched cruise missiles |
| **Special Operations Support** | Insert, support, and extract special operations forces via naval platforms |

!!! note "OR Lens — Power Projection"
    Amphibious assault planning involves force sequencing (integer programming) and beach landing throughput modeling (simulation). Carrier strike sortie generation is an **air tasking order** scheduling problem amenable to integer programming.

## Deterrence and Forward Presence Operations

Forward presence and deterrence operations demonstrate capability and resolve to prevent adversary aggression.

| Operation Type | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Strategic Deterrence Patrols (SSBN)** | Ballistic missile submarine (SSBN) patrols provide the sea-based leg of the nuclear triad; patrol areas and timing are carefully managed |
| **Freedom of Navigation Operations (FONOPS)** | Transit through waters claimed by states in a manner inconsistent with international law to assert navigational rights |
| **Theater Security Cooperation (TSC)** | Combined exercises, port visits, and training with partner navies to build interoperability and regional stability |
| **Show of Force** | Deploy naval forces conspicuously to signal resolve and deter adversary aggression |

---

## Humanitarian Assistance / Disaster Relief (HA/DR)

Naval forces have unique reach and sustainment capability to deliver humanitarian relief to populations affected by natural disasters or conflict.

| Operation Type | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Noncombatant Evacuation Operations (NEO)** | Evacuate U.S. citizens and designated foreign nationals from a hostile or potentially hostile environment using naval and amphibious assets |
| **Disaster Relief Operations** | Provide food, water, medical care, and shelter using naval hospital ships and afloat logistics platforms |
| **Maritime Search and Rescue (SAR)** | Locate and recover survivors of maritime accidents, aircraft ditchings, or disasters |

!!! note "OR Lens — HA/DR"
    Disaster relief logistics from naval platforms is a **vehicle routing and inventory management** problem. Hospital ship capacity and patient throughput can be modeled with queueing theory.

---

## Anti-Access / Area Denial (A2/AD) Counter-Operations

As adversaries develop sophisticated A2/AD capabilities, naval commanders must plan operations to degrade or defeat layered denial systems.

| Operation Type | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Distributed Maritime Operations (DMO)** | Disperse naval forces across a wide area to complicate adversary targeting while massing effects through long-range fires |
| **Littoral Operations in a Contested Environment (LOCE)** | Conduct sea control and power projection in heavily defended littoral zones using a combination of surface, subsurface, and air platforms |
| **Electronic Warfare and Deception** | Employ jamming, spoofing, and electromagnetic deception to defeat enemy sensors and disrupt A2/AD kill chains |

## Summary

| Operation Type | Primary Objective | Key Platforms |
|----------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Anti-Surface Warfare (ASuW) | Sink/neutralize enemy surface forces | Destroyers, aircraft, submarines |
| Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW) | Neutralize enemy submarines | P-8, helicopters, surface ships, SSN |
| Submarine Barrier Operations | Interdict enemy submarines/surface ships | SSN, SSK |
| Mine Warfare (MIW) | Control sea lanes | MCM ships, aircraft, EOD |
| Maritime Interdiction Operations | Enforce maritime law | Destroyers, helicopters, boarding teams |
| Carrier Strike Operations | Power projection ashore | CVN, CSG |
| Amphibious Operations | Seize objectives ashore | LHD/LHA, LPD, LCAC, Marines |
| SSBN Deterrence Patrol | Nuclear deterrence | SSBN |
| HA/DR / NEO | Protect/assist civilians | LHD, hospital ships, amphibious shipping |
| Distributed Maritime Operations | A2/AD penetration | All naval forces |
