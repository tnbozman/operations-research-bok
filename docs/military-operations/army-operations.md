# Army Operations

## Introduction

Army (land) forces are the ultimate arbiters of ground combat, providing the sustained presence needed to control territory, protect populations, and achieve lasting political outcomes. The U.S. Army's operational doctrine is codified in **ADP 3-0 (Operations)** and **FM 3-0 (Operations)**, which describe a unified land operations framework spanning offense, defense, stability, and civil support.

Force commanders at the corps, division, and brigade combat team (BCT) echelon task operational outcomes across the following primary operation types.

---

## Offensive Operations

Offensive operations defeat or destroy enemy forces and seize terrain to impose the commander's will on the adversary.

| Operation Type | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Movement to Contact** | Develop the situation and gain/regain contact with the enemy; used when enemy location is uncertain |
| **Attack** | Employ firepower and maneuver to destroy, neutralize, or capture the enemy; includes hasty and deliberate forms |
| **Exploitation** | Follow a successful attack to prevent the enemy from reconstituting and to expand gains |
| **Pursuit** | Catch and destroy a retreating enemy before they can establish a defense or withdraw to safety |
| **Raids** | Limited-objective attacks into enemy-controlled territory to destroy targets, capture personnel, or gather information; forces return to friendly lines after completion |
| **Feints and Demonstrations** | Deception operations that create uncertainty about the main effort |

!!! note "OR Lens — Offensive Operations"
    Key analytical questions: optimal axis of advance (routing/network flow), fire support allocation (integer programming), maneuver timing (schedule optimization), and assessment of enemy defensive capacity (Monte Carlo simulation).

---

## Defensive Operations

Defensive operations defeat attacking forces, preserve combat power, and buy time for conditions favorable to resuming the offense.

| Operation Type | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Area Defense** | Retain terrain by concentrating forces at decisive points; defenders anchor to key terrain |
| **Mobile Defense** | Use a combination of fires and movement to allow attacking enemy forces to enter a battle area and then defeat them with a decisive counterattack |
| **Retrograde Operations** | Organized movement away from the enemy: includes delay, withdrawal, and retirement |
| **Perimeter Defense** | All-around defense when a unit is isolated or surrounded |
| **Reserve Positioning and Commitment** | Maintaining and optimally committing uncommitted forces to influence the battle |

!!! note "OR Lens — Defensive Operations"
    Key analytical questions: optimal placement of defensive positions (facility location), delay-vs.-defend trade-offs (dynamic programming), and attrition modeling (Lanchester equations).

---

## Stability Operations

Stability operations re-establish a safe and secure environment, restore essential services, and develop the conditions needed for lasting peace.

| Operation Type | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Civil Security** | Establish security conditions to protect civilian populations from armed groups and criminal violence |
| **Civil Control** | Regulate civilian movement and behavior in an area of operations to reduce interference with military operations and protect the populace |
| **Restore Essential Services** | Provide or support the provision of essential civil services (water, electricity, health care, sanitation) |
| **Support to Governance** | Assist legitimate local authorities in re-establishing effective, representative governance |
| **Support to Economic and Infrastructure Development** | Rebuild physical and economic infrastructure to promote long-term stability |
| **Humanitarian Assistance / Disaster Relief (HA/DR)** | Provide relief to populations affected by natural disasters, famine, or conflict-induced displacement |

!!! note "OR Lens — Stability Operations"
    Key analytical questions: prioritization of reconstruction projects (multi-criteria decision analysis), resource allocation across competing stability lines of effort (linear programming), and population vulnerability assessment (spatial analysis/GIS).

---

## Large-Scale Combat Operations (LSCO)

LSCO is the Army's most demanding operational environment, involving sustained, high-intensity conflict against a peer or near-peer adversary with significant combined arms forces.

| Operation Type | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Decisive Action** | The continuous, simultaneous execution of offensive, defensive, and stability tasks |
| **Deep Operations** | Actions against enemy forces, functions, and capabilities before they can be brought to bear against friendly forces |
| **Close Operations** | Actions by committed forces to defeat enemy forces in direct contact |
| **Rear Operations** | Actions in the rear area to sustain the force and ensure freedom of action |
| **Shaping Operations** | Operations that create conditions for the decisive operation |
| **Sustaining Operations** | Logistics, maintenance, medical, and personnel operations that generate and maintain combat power |

!!! note "OR Lens — LSCO"
    Key analytical questions: logistics sustainment under attrition (simulation), targeting prioritization (multi-attribute value theory), synchronization of deep/close/rear operations (scheduling), and force regeneration modeling.

---

## Civil Support Operations

Civil support operations assist domestic civil authorities in responding to crises that exceed their capabilities.

| Operation Type | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Defense Support of Civil Authorities (DSCA)** | Support to federal, state, and local agencies during domestic emergencies |
| **Pandemic / CBRN Response** | Provide medical support, decontamination, and logistics during chemical, biological, radiological, nuclear, or infectious disease events |
| **Evacuation Operations** | Assist in the evacuation of non-combatants (NEO) or civilian populations from dangerous areas |
| **Search and Rescue** | Locate, extract, and provide initial medical treatment to isolated personnel |

## Summary

| Operation Type | Primary Objective | Typical Echelon |
|----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Movement to Contact | Develop situation | Brigade–Division |
| Attack (Hasty/Deliberate) | Destroy/seize | Battalion–Corps |
| Exploitation / Pursuit | Exploit success | Division–Corps |
| Area / Mobile Defense | Deny terrain / attrite | Brigade–Corps |
| Retrograde | Preserve force | Division–Corps |
| Stability Tasks | Restore conditions | Brigade–Division |
| HA/DR | Protect civilians | Division–Corps |
| LSCO / Decisive Action | Defeat adversary | Corps–Army |
| DSCA | Domestic support | Division–Corps |
