# Operations Research Body of Knowledge

Welcome to the **Operations Research Body of Knowledge (OR-BoK)** — a quick-start reference for engineers entering the field of operations research, with a particular focus on military and defense applications.

## What is Operations Research?

**Operations Research (OR)** is a discipline that applies advanced analytical methods to help make better decisions. It draws on mathematics, statistics, optimization, simulation, and data science to analyze complex systems and support decision-makers in achieving their objectives efficiently.

Key technique areas include:

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Linear Programming** | Optimizing a linear objective function subject to linear constraints |
| **Integer Programming** | Optimization where some or all variables are constrained to integer values |
| **Network Analysis** | Modeling flows, routing, and connectivity in networked systems |
| **Simulation** | Modeling stochastic systems to estimate performance and risk |
| **Queueing Theory** | Analyzing waiting lines and service systems |
| **Decision Analysis** | Structuring decisions under uncertainty using probability and utility theory |
| **Game Theory** | Modeling strategic interactions between rational agents |
| **Heuristics & Metaheuristics** | Approximate methods for large or intractable optimization problems |
| **Stochastic Processes** | Modeling systems that evolve probabilistically over time |

Explore each technique in depth — with lectures, code examples, and hands-on exercises — in the [**OR Techniques**](techniques/linear-programming/index.md) section. A reusable [**Prompt Template**](techniques/prompt-template.md) is provided to generate interactive learning content for any topic.

## Why Operations Research for Military Applications?

Military operations involve some of the most complex decision-making environments imaginable: resource constraints, adversarial uncertainty, time pressure, and high stakes. OR techniques have been applied to military problems since World War II — ranging from convoy routing and logistics to force employment and campaign analysis.

Force commanders routinely rely on OR-informed analysis to:

- Allocate limited forces and resources across competing tasks
- Schedule logistics and sustainment pipelines
- Assess risk and forecast operational outcomes
- Plan maneuver schemes and targeting sequences
- Evaluate courses of action (COAs) in wargames and exercises
